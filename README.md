# HR Analytics Dashboard (Power BI)

A comprehensive Power BI dashboard designed to analyze employee data, track attrition rates, and uncover key workforce insights to help HR teams make data-driven decisions.

##  Project Overview

This dashboard provides a deep dive into organizational health by analyzing employee demographics, department distributions, salary structures, and attrition patterns. It helps stakeholders understand *why* and *where* employee turnover is happening.

### Key Metrics Tracked:
* **Total Employees:** 1,470
* **Total Attrition:** 237
* **Attrition Rate:** 16.1%
* **Average Salary:** 6.5K
* **Average Age:** 37 Years
* **Average Years at Company:** 7.0 Years

---

##  Key Insights & Visualizations

The dashboard is divided into several analytical sections:

1. **Demographics & Gender:** Explores workforce distribution by gender, highlighting the split between Male (858) and Female (577) employees across the organization.
2. **Education Field:** Visualizes attrition across different backgrounds, highlighting **Life Sciences (41%)** and **Medical (32%)** as the sectors with the highest turnover.
3. **Age Groups:** Tracks turnover across generations, showing peak attrition in the **26–35** age bracket, followed by **36–45**.
4. **Salary Slab Analysis:** Groups employees into income brackets (Upto 5k, 5k–10k, 10k–15k, 15k+). The data reveals that the highest attrition happens in the lower income bracket (**Upto 5k** with 749 employees).
5. **Tenure (Years at Company):** An area chart showing attrition spikes during the early years of service, particularly peaking between **1 to 5 years** of tenure.
6. **Job Role Breakdown:** A detailed matrix and horizontal bar chart identifying which roles experience the most turnover, with **Sales Executive (326)**, **Research Scientist (292)**, and **Laboratory Technician (259)** leading the metrics.

## How to Use/View the Report

1. Clone or download this repository.
2. Ensure you have **Power BI Desktop** installed.
3. Open the `.pbix` file (e.g., `HR_Analytics_Dashboard.pbix`).
4. Use the top interactive slicers (**Human Resources**, **Research & Development**, **Sales**) to dynamically filter the entire dashboard by specific departments.
